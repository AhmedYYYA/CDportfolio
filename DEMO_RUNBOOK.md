# Executive Demonstration Runbook

## Demonstration statement

Begin with this statement:

> This is an unofficial demonstration using synthetic, non-operational data. It illustrates a potential aviation capability governance and decision-support environment. It is not connected to any operational organization or system.

## Three-minute command journey

### 0:00–0:20 — Trust boundary

- Point to the synthetic demonstration badge, reporting date, language, and selected role.
- Explain that public reference structures and synthetic operational records are deliberately separated.

### 0:20–0:45 — Command overview

- Show the selected demonstration-role context and the derived threshold-attainment, programme, gate and critical-action measures.
- Point to the command-attention queue and state that a critical assurance blocker cannot be averaged into a green portfolio score.

### 0:45–1:25 — Challengeable assurance

- Open **Lifecycle & Assurance** and scroll to the challengeable synthetic assurance chain.
- Select **After re-test**: show separate performance/safety requirements, the ground/fictional-flight sequence, the bounded evidence limitation and conditional G4 eligibility.
- Select **After in-service occurrence**: show how the repeated fictional control breach reopens the finding and returns G4 to blocked.
- State that this is deterministic demonstration logic, not airworthiness approval or authorization to test.

### 1:25–1:55 — Mission-to-transition thread

- In the same workspace, open the maritime-search/MR-90 mission-to-transition explorer.
- Move through the requirement, configuration, hazard, test/evidence, simulated decision, sustainment, cost/benefit and transition records.
- Use the predecessor/successor links to demonstrate bidirectional traceability and the adjacent non-authority boundary.

### 1:55–2:25 — Readiness recovery scenario

- Open **Readiness & Resources** and distinguish mission-capable readiness from technical serviceability.
- Adjust the MR-90 repair lead-time and repair-slot assumptions.
- Compare baseline with deterministic illustrative P50/P90 time, readiness and 90-day cost outcomes.
- Open the constraint details to show the linked fictional assets, risk, action and scheduled decision.

### 2:25–2:45 — Role-specific work

- Open My Work as programme manager and show the gate-pack preparation queue.
- Switch to independent test or airworthiness and show that the visible assurance actions, role context, and queue count change; optionally switch to OEM / FSR to show its single assigned fictional case.
- State clearly that these differences are client-side presentation filters over synthetic records. They demonstrate intended journeys but do not authenticate a person, hide bundled data, authorize an action, or enforce segregation; a production implementation would enforce those controls on the server.

### 2:45–3:00 — Bilingual assurance

- Switch to Arabic without losing route or context.
- Show the mirrored RTL layout and unchanged identifiers, units, formulas, and evidence.

## Demonstration guardrails

- Do not imply official endorsement, operational adoption, airworthiness approval, or OEM validation.
- Do not substitute real force structure, readiness, costs, contracts, faults, stock exposure, personnel, or locations.
- Do not describe simulated roles as security controls.
- Do not call generated evidence or readiness values “approved.”
- If asked about production, explain the controlled-pilot architecture and approval prerequisites.
